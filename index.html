How the Process Works

A Practice Limited to Full Arch Rehabilitation

We focus exclusively on implant-led full arch treatment. This allows us to refine our approach, invest in specialised workflows, and accept only cases where planning makes the difference.

Complete replacement of a full arch using implants to support a fixed bridge. Every case begins with diagnostic imaging and three-dimensional planning to determine the most predictable approach.

Why Patients Choose This Approach

Full arch treatment requires more than technical skill. It requires experience with complex cases, access to planning technology, and a willingness to say no when the timing isn't right.

Everything Under One Roof

The Treatment Pathway

From initial assessment to long-term maintenance, the process is structured, methodical, and designed around predictability. Timelines vary depending on individual anatomy and healing response.

1

Initial Assessment

A detailed consultation to understand your concerns, examine existing dentition or dentures, and discuss whether full arch treatment aligns with your goals. Honest advice on suitability and timing.

2

Diagnostic Planning

CBCT imaging reveals bone anatomy in three dimensions. Digital planning software determines implant positions, angulations, and prosthetic design before any surgical work begins.

3

Implant Placement

Implants are placed using guided surgery for precision. In most cases, a provisional fixed bridge is fitted the same day, though immediate loading is not always clinically appropriate.

4

Integration Period

Implants integrate with bone over three to six months. During this time, provisional teeth remain in place and you attend regular review appointments to monitor healing and function.

5

Final Restoration

Once integration is confirmed, your definitive bridge is designed, fabricated, and fitted. Materials and aesthetics are chosen based on your preferences and clinical requirements.

6

Ongoing Maintenance

Long-term success depends on regular professional maintenance. Implants require hygiene protocols and periodic review to ensure tissue health and prosthetic integrity over decades.
