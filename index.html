§ Questions

The ones we hear most often.

How long does treatment take from start to finish? +
Typically three to six months for a full arch. Single implants can be quicker. A provisional set of teeth is usually fitted on the day of surgery; the final zirconia prosthesis is fitted once the implants have fully integrated with the bone.
Will I be without teeth at any point? +
No. We fit a provisional fixed prosthesis on the day of implant placement in most cases. You leave the clinic with teeth the same day. The final prosthesis replaces the provisional once healing is complete.
Is it painful? +
The surgery itself is carried out under local anaesthetic, with sedation available. Most patients describe the recovery as less uncomfortable than a typical tooth extraction, and are back at work within 48 hours.
What if I don't have enough bone? +
We identify this at the CBCT scan stage. Where bone grafting is required it is included in the written treatment plan and fixed-price quote. In most cases it is carried out alongside the main surgery.
How long do implants last? +
Properly placed and maintained, dental implants routinely last decades. Long-term studies — including the Pjetursson and Moraschini systematic reviews — report implant survival rates of around 95–97% at five years and 90–95% at ten years across modern implant systems. We use the same modern systems and protocols. The zirconia prosthesis itself is extremely stable and resists staining indefinitely, though periodic maintenance is expected.
Do you offer finance? +
Yes. We offer 0% interest over 12 months, and low-rate finance (9.9% APR representative) over up to 60 months. Both are subject to credit approval. Full details on the pricing page.
