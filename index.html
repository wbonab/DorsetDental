Assessment Determines the Right Path

Not every patient requires full arch treatment. Our focus is on matching intervention to clinical need—whether that means targeted implant placement for early tooth loss or comprehensive rehabilitation when multiple teeth have failed.

Full Arch Implant Rehabilitation

When multiple teeth have failed or dentures are no longer viable, full arch treatment provides a fixed, stable foundation. This is where planning matters most—implant positions, bone grafting decisions, and prosthetic design are mapped digitally before surgery begins.

The Treatment Pathway

Whether your case requires targeted implant placement or comprehensive full arch rehabilitation, the process begins with diagnostic assessment and ends with long-term maintenance planning.

1

Initial Assessment

Clinical examination, radiographic imaging, and discussion of your concerns. We determine what level of intervention is indicated and whether full arch treatment, targeted implants, or a different approach makes the most sense.

2

Diagnostic Planning

CBCT scans reveal bone anatomy in three dimensions. Digital planning determines implant positions, identifies grafting requirements, and maps prosthetic design. The extent of planning depends on case complexity.

3

Surgical Phase

Implants are placed using guided surgery where indicated. For full arch cases, provisional fixed restorations are typically provided the same day. Targeted implant cases may involve delayed or immediate loading depending on clinical conditions.

4

Integration Period

Implants integrate with bone over three to six months. Full arch patients wear provisional fixed teeth throughout. Targeted implant patients may have temporary restorations or healing caps, depending on the protocol used.

5

Final Restoration

Once integration is confirmed, definitive restorations are fabricated and fitted. For full arch cases, this involves custom-designed fixed bridges. For targeted implants, individual crowns or bridges are delivered.

6

Long-Term Maintenance

All implant cases require ongoing professional maintenance and hygiene protocols. Regular review appointments monitor tissue health, prosthetic integrity, and occlusal stability over years and decades.
