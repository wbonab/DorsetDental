What Are Full Arch Dental Implants?

Full arch dental implants replace an entire upper or lower arch of teeth using just 4-6 strategically placed implants. This technique—often called "All-on-4" or "All-on-X"—allows us to provide you with a complete set of fixed teeth in a single day.

Unlike traditional dentures that rest on your gums and require adhesives, full arch implants are permanently fixed in place. They look, feel, and function like natural teeth—allowing you to eat, speak, and smile with complete confidence.

How Does the All-on-4/All-on-X Technique Work?

The secret to full arch implants lies in strategic implant placement:

  1. Strategic Positioning: We place 4-6 implants at precise angles in areas of your jaw with the best bone density.
  2. Immediate Loading: A temporary bridge is attached to the implants the same day, so you're never without teeth.
  3. Optimal Support: The angled placement distributes forces evenly, providing exceptional stability.
  4. Bone Preservation: Implants stimulate the bone, maintaining your facial structure.

This approach often eliminates the need for bone grafting, reduces treatment time, and provides predictable, long-lasting results.

Are You a Candidate for Full Arch Implants?

Full arch dental implants are suitable for many people, but candidacy depends on individual factors. You may be a good candidate if:

  • You're missing most or all teeth in one or both arches
  • Your remaining teeth are failing and beyond repair
  • You wear dentures and want a permanent solution
  • You have adequate bone density (or are willing to consider bone grafting)
  • You're in good general health
  • You don't smoke, or you're willing to quit
  • You're committed to maintaining excellent oral hygiene

During your consultation, we'll assess your oral health, bone structure, and medical history to determine if full arch implants are right for you. If additional procedures like bone grafting are needed, we'll discuss your options.

What to Expect: The Treatment Process

1. Initial Consultation (60-90 minutes)

We begin with a comprehensive examination including:

  • Detailed discussion of your concerns and goals
  • 3D CT scan to assess bone quality and quantity
  • Digital photos and impressions
  • Treatment plan development
  • Transparent cost breakdown

2. Treatment Planning (1-2 weeks)

Using advanced software, we digitally plan your treatment:

  • Virtual implant placement for optimal positioning
  • Custom surgical guide fabrication
  • Temporary teeth design and fabrication
  • Final treatment timeline confirmation

3. Surgery Day (3-4 hours)

The day you receive your new teeth:

  • Any remaining teeth are removed (if needed)
  • Implants are placed using guided surgery
  • Temporary fixed bridge is attached
  • Post-operative instructions and medications provided

4. Healing Phase (3-6 months)

While you wear your comfortable temporary teeth:

  • Implants integrate with your jawbone (osseointegration)
  • Regular check-ups to monitor healing
  • Any necessary adjustments to temporary teeth
  • Progressive return to normal diet

5. Final Teeth Placement (2-3 appointments)

Once healing is complete:

  • Impressions for your permanent bridge
  • Try-in appointments to perfect fit and appearance
  • Final bridge placement and bite adjustment
  • Care instructions and maintenance schedule

Recovery and Aftercare

What to Expect After Surgery

Most patients experience:

  • Minimal discomfort: Manageable with prescribed or over-the-counter pain medication
  • Some swelling: Peaks around day 3, then gradually subsides
  • Soft diet: Required for 4-6 weeks to protect the implants
  • Quick recovery: Most return to work within 2-3 days

Long-Term Care

To ensure your implants last decades:

  • Brush and floss daily (we'll show you how)
  • Use a water flosser to clean under the bridge
  • Attend regular check-ups every 6 months
  • Have professional cleanings by trained hygienists
  • Avoid smoking, which can compromise implants

How Long Do Full Arch Implants Last?

With proper care, dental implants can last 20+ years—often a lifetime. The bridge itself (your visible teeth) typically lasts 10-15 years before needing replacement, though the implants remain in place.

Factors affecting longevity include:

  • Oral hygiene practices
  • Regular professional maintenance
  • Smoking status
  • General health conditions
  • Initial implant placement quality

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the procedure hurt?

The surgery is performed under local anaesthetic, so you won't feel pain during the procedure. Most patients report that discomfort after surgery is less than they expected and is easily managed with medication. Many compare it to having several teeth extracted.

Will I be without teeth during treatment?

No. You'll receive temporary fixed teeth on the same day as your implant surgery. You're never left without teeth.

Can I get both arches done at once?

In some cases, yes. However, we often recommend treating one arch at a time to make recovery easier and ensure you can eat comfortably. We'll discuss the best approach for your situation.

What if I don't have enough bone?

The All-on-4 technique is designed to work in areas of the jaw with the best available bone, often eliminating the need for grafting. If grafting is needed, we'll discuss your options, including advanced techniques like zygomatic implants.

How soon can I eat normally?

You'll need to stick to a soft diet for 4-6 weeks while the implants integrate. After that, you can gradually return to eating all your favourite foods. Once fully healed, there are no dietary restrictions.

What's the success rate?

Full arch implants have an excellent success rate—over 95% when placed by experienced clinicians. Success depends on factors like bone quality, oral hygiene, smoking status, and overall health.

Am I too old for dental implants?

Age alone is not a limiting factor. We've successfully treated patients well into their 80s. What matters more is your overall health, bone density, and healing capacity. If you're healthy enough for a tooth extraction, you're likely healthy enough for implants.

Can I have sedation?

Yes. We can arrange sedation options for anxious patients. This can range from oral sedation to IV sedation, depending on your needs and preferences.
