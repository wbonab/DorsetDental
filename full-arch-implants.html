What Are Full Arch Dental Implants?

Full arch dental implants are a revolutionary solution for people who are missing most or all of their teeth in the upper or lower jaw (or both). Rather than replacing each tooth individually, full arch implant treatment uses just 4-6 strategically placed dental implants to support a complete set of fixed, permanent teeth.

This treatment is often called "All-on-4" or "All-on-6" depending on how many implants are used. The implants act as strong, stable anchors that are surgically placed into your jawbone. Once healed, they fuse with the bone and provide a permanent foundation for your new teeth.

Unlike removable dentures, full arch implants are fixed in place. They look, feel, and function just like natural teeth. You can eat, speak, smile, and live your life with complete confidence.

Key Benefits of Full Arch Implants

  • Fixed, permanent teeth that never come out

  • No adhesives, no slippage, no embarrassment
  • Can often be completed in a single day

Who Needs Full Arch Dental Implants?

Full arch dental implants may be the ideal solution if you:

Are Missing Most or All Teeth

If you've lost multiple teeth due to decay, gum disease, injury, or other causes, full arch implants can restore your entire smile.

Wear Dentures

Tired of loose, uncomfortable dentures that slip, click, or require messy adhesives? Full arch implants provide a permanent alternative.

Have Failing Teeth

If your remaining teeth are severely damaged, loose, or beyond repair, we can remove them and replace your entire arch with strong, healthy implant teeth.

Want a Permanent Solution

If you're looking for a long-term solution that feels and functions like natural teeth, full arch implants offer unmatched stability and confidence.

Treatment for Failing Teeth

Many patients come to us with teeth that are loose, painful, infected, or failing due to advanced gum disease or severe decay. In these cases, trying to save individual teeth may not be practical or in your best long-term interest.

Full arch implant treatment offers a complete "reset" for your oral health. We can:

  • Remove severely compromised teeth that are causing pain or infection
  • Eliminate chronic dental problems
  • Replace your entire arch with strong, permanent implant teeth
  • Restore your smile, function, and confidence in a single comprehensive treatment

This approach is often more cost-effective, less invasive, and more predictable than attempting multiple individual tooth-saving procedures that may ultimately fail.

The Treatment Process: Step by Step

1

Initial Consultation

Your journey begins with a comprehensive consultation. We'll discuss your concerns, medical history, and treatment goals. A clinical examination and 3D imaging (CBCT scan) allow us to assess your jawbone quality and plan your treatment with precision.

This consultation is free and carries no obligation.

2

Treatment Planning & Design

Using advanced digital technology, we create a detailed 3D plan showing exactly where your implants will be placed and how your new teeth will look. You'll see a preview of your finished smile before treatment even begins.

This planning phase ensures precision, safety, and optimal aesthetic results.

3

Surgery Day: Implant Placement & Provisional Teeth

On your surgery day, we'll place your dental implants using a minimally invasive technique. If you have any remaining teeth that need removal, we'll do this at the same time. Once your implants are in place, we immediately attach a provisional (temporary) set of teeth.

You'll leave the same day with a brand new, functional smile.

4

Healing Period

Over the next 3-6 months, your implants will integrate with your jawbone through a natural process called osseointegration. During this time, you'll wear your provisional teeth, allowing you to eat, speak, and smile normally while your implants heal.

Regular check-ups ensure everything is progressing as expected.

5

Final Teeth

Once your implants have fully integrated, we'll create your final, permanent teeth. These are custom-crafted to match your facial features, bite, and aesthetic preferences. They're made from high-strength materials designed to last for decades.

Your beautiful, permanent smile is complete.

6

Ongoing Care

With proper care and regular maintenance visits, your full arch implants can last 20 years or more. We'll provide you with detailed care instructions and see you regularly to ensure your implants remain healthy and functional.

We're committed to your long-term success.

Recovery & What to Expect

Most patients are surprised by how comfortable the recovery process is. Here's what you can expect:

Immediate Post-Surgery (Days 1-3)

  • Some swelling and mild discomfort (easily managed with over-the-counter pain relief)
  • Soft food diet recommended
  • Most patients return to work within 2-3 days
  • Your provisional teeth are in place and functional

First Two Weeks

  • Swelling subsides
  • Gradually introduce more foods
  • Follow-up appointment to check healing
  • Resume normal activities

Healing Period (3-6 Months)

  • Implants integrate with bone
  • Continue wearing provisional teeth
  • Return to full normal diet (with provisional teeth)
  • Regular check-ups to monitor progress

Important Note

While full arch implant surgery is generally well-tolerated, all surgical procedures carry some risks. These include infection, nerve damage, implant failure, and complications related to anaesthesia. Your dentist will discuss these risks with you in detail during your consultation. Individual results and recovery times vary.

Longevity & Maintenance

Full arch dental implants are designed to be a long-term solution. With proper care, they can last 20 years or moreâ€”often for the rest of your life.

Daily Care

Caring for your implant teeth is similar to caring for natural teeth:

  • Brush twice daily with a soft toothbrush
  • Use interdental brushes or water flossers to clean around implants
  • Avoid smoking (it can compromise implant success)
  • Maintain a healthy diet

Professional Maintenance

Regular visits to our practice (typically every 6 months) allow us to:

  • Professionally clean your implants and teeth
  • Check for any signs of complications
  • Make any necessary adjustments
  • Ensure your bite remains balanced

With diligent home care and regular professional maintenance, full arch dental implants have an excellent long-term success rate, with clinical studies showing survival rates above 95% at 10 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the treatment take?

The entire process typically takes 3-6 months from consultation to final teeth. However, you'll receive provisional teeth on the same day as your surgery, so you're never without teeth.

Is the procedure painful?

The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia, so you won't feel pain during surgery. Most patients report minimal discomfort afterwards, easily managed with over-the-counter pain relief. Many are surprised by how comfortable the process is.

Am I too old for dental implants?

Age is rarely a barrier. As long as you're in reasonable general health, you can benefit from dental implants. We successfully treat patients in their 70s, 80s, and beyond.

What if I don't have enough bone?

The All-on-4 and All-on-6 techniques are specifically designed to work even with reduced bone volume. In some cases, bone grafting may be required, but many patients with bone loss are still suitable candidates. A consultation with a 3D scan will determine your options.

Can I eat normally with full arch implants?

Yes! Once fully healed, you can eat virtually anything you want. Unlike dentures, there are no dietary restrictions. Bite into an apple, enjoy steak, or eat crunchy foods without worry.

How do I clean my implant teeth?

You brush and floss them much like natural teeth. We'll provide specific instructions and may recommend special brushes or water flossers to keep the areas around your implants clean. Regular professional cleanings are also important.

What's the success rate?

Full arch dental implants have excellent success ratesâ€”typically above 95% at 10 years when placed by experienced clinicians and properly maintained by patients.

Can I have sedation?

Yes. We offer various sedation options to ensure your comfort, including intravenous (IV) sedation. We'll discuss your options during your consultation.

Do you offer finance?

Yes. We work with reputable healthcare finance providers to offer flexible payment plans, including 0% finance options for qualified patients. We want to make treatment affordable and accessible.

What if an implant fails?

While rare, implant failure can occur. If this happens within the first year, we typically replace the implant at no additional cost. Your treatment plan will include details about our warranty and what's covered.
