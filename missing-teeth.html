Living with Missing or Failing Teeth

Missing teeth or failing teeth affect more than just your smile. They impact your ability to eat, speak, and feel confident in social situations. Over time, they can lead to bone loss, shifting teeth, and changes to your facial structure.

If you're dealing with any of these situations, full arch dental implants may be the comprehensive solution you've been looking for:

  • You're missing most or all of your teeth
  • You have multiple failing, loose, or broken teeth
  • Your teeth are severely decayed and beyond repair
  • You're wearing dentures and want a permanent solution
  • You've been told you need multiple extractions
  • You're embarrassed by the condition of your teeth

What Are Full Arch Implants?

Full arch dental implants replace all the teeth in your upper or lower jaw with a complete set of permanently fixed teeth supported by just 4-6 dental implants.

The Process in Brief:

  • Any remaining failing teeth are removed
  • 4-6 implants are placed in your jawbone
  • A full arch of temporary fixed teeth is attached the same day
  • After 3-6 months of healing, your permanent teeth are fitted

When Should You Consider This Treatment?

Full arch implants are ideal if you're in any of these situations:

✓ Multiple Failing Teeth

If you have numerous teeth that are decayed, broken, or loose, full arch treatment is often more practical than trying to save individual teeth.

✓ Advanced Gum Disease

Severe periodontal disease has loosened multiple teeth and damaged the supporting bone.

✓ Extensive Decay

Multiple teeth are beyond repair due to decay or failed previous dental work.

✓ Already Wearing Dentures

You're tired of removable dentures and want the security of fixed teeth.

✓ Dental Anxiety

Years of avoiding the dentist have left your teeth in poor condition. You want a fresh start.

✓ Compromised Function

Your teeth no longer allow you to eat properly or speak clearly.

Is It Too Late for Me?

Many patients with failing teeth assume it's "too late" to do anything about it. This is rarely true.

Even if your teeth are in poor condition, even if you've lost significant bone, even if you've been told you're not a candidate for dental work—full arch implants may still be possible.

Common Misconceptions:

  • "I've lost too much bone" → The All-on-4 technique works even with reduced bone
  • "I'm too old" → Age is not a barrier; we treat patients in their 70s, 80s, and 90s
  • "My teeth are too far gone" → That's exactly why full arch treatment exists
  • "It's too expensive" → Finance options make treatment more affordable
