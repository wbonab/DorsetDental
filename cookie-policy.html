Cookie Policy

How we use cookies and similar technologies on our website.

Last Updated: February 2026

Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Bournemouth Implants ("we", "us", "our") uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website (bournemouthimplants.com). By using our website, you consent to our use of cookies in accordance with this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies typically contain:

  • The name of the website that created the cookie
  • A unique identifier
  • An expiration date
  • Information about your browsing activity

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Ensure our website functions properly
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Understand how you use our website
  • Improve your browsing experience
  • Provide relevant information and content
  • Measure the effectiveness of our website

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

Purpose: Essential for the website to function properly
Examples: Security, navigation, basic functionality
Can be disabled: No (website won't work without them)

These cookies are essential for you to browse the website and use its features. Without these cookies, services like secure areas and contact forms cannot be provided.

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
session_id Maintains your session while browsing Session (deleted when browser closes)
cookie_consent Remembers your cookie preferences 1 year

3.2 Performance Cookies

Purpose: Collect information about how you use our website
Examples: Google Analytics (if implemented)
Can be disabled: Yes

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often. This data is aggregated and anonymous. We use this information to improve how our website works.

3.3 Functionality Cookies

Purpose: Remember your choices and preferences
Examples: Language settings, region preferences
Can be disabled: Yes

These cookies allow the website to remember choices you make (such as your preferred settings) to provide enhanced, more personalized features.

4. First-Party vs. Third-Party Cookies

4.1 First-Party Cookies

These are cookies set by our website directly. We have full control over these cookies and how they're used.

4.2 Third-Party Cookies

These are cookies set by external services we use on our website, such as:

  • Google Analytics: To understand website traffic and user behavior
  • Social Media Plugins: If you share content via social media buttons

Third-party cookies are governed by the privacy policies of those external services.

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have several options for managing cookies:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Delete cookies after each browsing session
  • Accept or reject cookies on a case-by-case basis

How to manage cookies in common browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data

For detailed instructions, visit: www.aboutcookies.org

5.2 Impact of Blocking Cookies

Please note that blocking certain cookies may impact your experience on our website:

  • Some features may not work properly
  • You may need to re-enter information
  • Website performance may be affected
  • We won't be able to remember your preferences

Strictly necessary cookies cannot be disabled as they're essential for the website to function.

6. Session vs. Persistent Cookies

6.1 Session Cookies

These are temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. They're used to maintain your session as you navigate our website.

6.2 Persistent Cookies

These remain on your device for a set period (or until you delete them). They're used to remember your preferences for future visits.

7. Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other technologies to track your website usage:

7.1 Web Beacons (Pixels)

Tiny transparent images embedded in web pages or emails that track whether you've viewed content.

7.2 Local Storage

Browser storage that allows us to store information locally on your device for improved performance.

7.3 Session Storage

Temporary storage that is cleared when you close your browser.

8. Mobile Devices

Our website is responsive and works on mobile devices. Mobile browsers also support cookies, and you can manage them through your device settings:

iOS (iPhone/iPad):

Settings → Safari → Block All Cookies

Android:

Chrome → Settings → Site Settings → Cookies

9. Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that signals websites not to track you. Currently, there is no universal standard for responding to DNT signals. We do not currently respond to DNT signals, but we respect your cookie choices made through our cookie consent banner or browser settings.

10. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect:

  • Changes in the cookies we use
  • New features or services on our website
  • Changes in regulations or best practices

When we make significant changes, we will notify you by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

11. More Information

For more information about how we collect, use, and protect your personal data, please see our Privacy Policy.
