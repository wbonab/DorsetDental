Complaints Procedure

We take your concerns seriously and aim to resolve any issues quickly and fairly.

Last Updated: February 2026

Our Commitment to You

At Bournemouth Implants, we strive to provide the highest standard of care and service. However, we recognize that occasionally things may not meet your expectations. When this happens, we want to hear from you.

We are committed to:

  • Listening to your concerns with respect and understanding
  • Investigating complaints thoroughly and fairly
  • Responding promptly and keeping you informed
  • Learning from feedback to improve our services
  • Resolving issues to your satisfaction wherever possible

What Can You Complain About?

You can raise a complaint about any aspect of our service, including:

  • Clinical treatment or care received
  • Behaviour or attitude of staff members
  • Communication or information provided
  • Appointment scheduling or waiting times
  • Practice facilities or cleanliness
  • Billing or payment matters
  • Any other aspect of your experience with us

How to Make a Complaint

We want to make it as easy as possible for you to raise concerns. You can contact us in several ways:

1. Speak to Us Directly

Many concerns can be resolved quickly through a conversation. Please speak to any member of staff, who will either address your concern immediately or arrange for an appropriate person to contact you.

What Information Should You Include?

To help us investigate your complaint effectively, please include:

  • Your full name and contact details
  • Your date of birth (for patient identification)
  • Date(s) when the issue occurred
  • Details of your complaint â€“ what happened and why you are dissatisfied
  • Names of any staff members involved (if known)
  • What outcome you would like from your complaint
  • Any supporting evidence (emails, photos, receipts, etc.)

Our Complaints Procedure

1

Acknowledgment (Within 3 Working Days)

We will acknowledge receipt of your complaint within three working days. You'll receive confirmation that we've received your complaint and an explanation of what happens next.

2

Investigation (Within 10 Working Days)

We will thoroughly investigate your complaint, which may include:

  • Reviewing relevant records and documentation
  • Speaking to staff members involved
  • Gathering all relevant information
  • Consulting with clinical experts if necessary

If we need more time, we'll let you know and keep you updated on progress.

3

Response (Within 20 Working Days)

We aim to provide a full written response within 20 working days. Our response will include:

  • An explanation of what happened
  • Our findings from the investigation
  • An apology if things went wrong
  • Details of any action we're taking
  • Information about further steps if you remain dissatisfied
4

Resolution

We hope our response resolves your concerns. If you're satisfied with the outcome, no further action is needed. If not, we'll arrange a meeting to discuss the matter further or explain your options for escalation.

What If You're Still Not Satisfied?

If you remain dissatisfied after we've investigated your complaint, you have several options:

1. Request a Review

You can request that a senior member of our team reviews the complaint and our response. This provides a fresh perspective on the matter.

Anonymous Complaints

While we prefer to discuss concerns directly with you, we understand you may wish to remain anonymous. We will still investigate anonymous complaints to the best of our ability, but please be aware that without your contact details, we cannot provide you with a response or update you on progress.

Learning from Complaints

We view complaints as valuable feedback that helps us improve. Every complaint is reviewed to identify:

  • What went wrong and why
  • Whether there are wider implications
  • What we can learn
  • What changes we should make

We regularly analyze complaints to identify trends and make improvements to our services, training, and procedures.

Your Rights

When you make a complaint, you have the right to:

  • Be treated with respect and courtesy
  • Have your complaint investigated fairly and thoroughly
  • Receive a timely response
  • Be kept informed of progress
  • Have your complaint handled confidentially
  • Bring someone with you to meetings (advocate, friend, or family member)
  • Escalate your complaint if you're not satisfied

Time Limits

We encourage you to raise complaints as soon as possible after the issue occurs. This helps us investigate while events are fresh in everyone's memory. Ideally, complaints should be made within:

  • 12 months of the incident
  • 12 months of becoming aware there was a problem

However, we will consider complaints outside these timeframes in exceptional circumstances.
