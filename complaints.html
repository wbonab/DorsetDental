Complaints Procedure

We are committed to providing excellent care and resolving any concerns you may have

Last Updated: February 8, 2026

At Bournemouth Dental Implants, we are committed to providing high-quality dental care and excellent patient service. We value your feedback and take all complaints seriously. If you are unhappy with any aspect of our service, we want to hear from you so we can put things right.

Our Commitment to You

  • We will listen to your concerns carefully and respectfully
  • We will investigate all complaints thoroughly and fairly
  • We will respond to complaints promptly
  • We will keep you informed throughout the process
  • We will learn from complaints to improve our services
  • We will treat all complaints confidentially

How to Make a Complaint

You can make a complaint in several ways:

1. Speak to Us Directly

Often, concerns can be resolved quickly by speaking directly with a member of our team. Please feel free to raise any issues with:

  • Your treating dentist
  • Our practice manager
  • Any member of our reception team

We encourage you to speak with us first, as many concerns can be resolved immediately through open communication.

2. In Writing

If you prefer to make a formal written complaint, you can:

By Email:

What Information Should You Include?

To help us investigate your complaint effectively, please include:

  • Your full name and contact details
  • Your date of birth (for patient identification)
  • Date(s) when the issue occurred
  • Details of your complaint â€“ what happened and why you are dissatisfied
  • Names of any staff members involved (if known)
  • What outcome you would like from your complaint
  • Any supporting evidence (emails, photos, receipts, etc.)

Our Complaints Procedure

1

Acknowledgment (Within 3 Working Days)

We will acknowledge receipt of your complaint within three working days. This acknowledgment will include:

  • Confirmation that we have received your complaint
  • Name of the person handling your complaint
  • Expected timeframe for our response
2

Investigation (Within 10 Working Days)

We will thoroughly investigate your complaint by:

  • Reviewing relevant patient records and documentation
  • Speaking with staff members involved
  • Gathering all relevant information
  • Consulting with clinical experts if necessary

If we need more time for a complex investigation, we will let you know and provide a revised timeframe.

3

Response (Within 20 Working Days)

We aim to provide a full written response within 20 working days of receiving your complaint. Our response will include:

  • A summary of your complaint
  • Details of our investigation
  • Our findings and conclusions
  • An explanation of any failings identified
  • An apology if appropriate
  • Details of any remedial action we will take
  • Information about further steps if you remain dissatisfied
4

Resolution and Follow-Up

We hope our response will resolve your complaint satisfactorily. If you accept our response:

  • We will implement any agreed actions
  • We will use your feedback to improve our services
  • We will follow up to ensure you are satisfied with the outcome

If you remain dissatisfied, we will explain the further options available to you (see below).

If You Are Still Not Satisfied

If you have followed our complaints procedure and remain unhappy with our response, you have the right to take your complaint to independent bodies:

1. NHS England (If NHS Treatment)

If your complaint relates to NHS treatment, you can contact NHS England:

Confidentiality

All complaints are handled confidentially. Information about your complaint will only be shared with:

  • Staff members directly involved in investigating or resolving the complaint
  • Our professional indemnity insurers (if necessary)
  • Regulatory bodies (if required by law or regulation)

We will always maintain your privacy and handle your personal information in accordance with our Privacy Policy and UK GDPR.

Learning from Complaints

We view complaints as opportunities to improve our services. We will:

  • Analyze complaint trends to identify areas for improvement
  • Implement changes to prevent similar issues occurring
  • Share learning with all staff members
  • Review our complaints procedure annually
  • Report on complaints and improvements to our practice team
