§ Pricing questions

Common questions about the fee.

Why is there an upfront consultation fee if my CBCT scan is free at the supermarket optician?

A CBCT scan is a high-dose 3D X-ray and must be interpreted by a clinician qualified to place implants into the structures it reveals. Our fee covers the scan, the examination, and the written clinical opinion that follows. If you proceed with treatment the £150 is offset against the final fee.

Will the final bill match the written quote?

Yes. The written treatment plan given to you after consultation is a fixed-price quote. It does not change unless the clinical situation itself changes materially — and any such change is discussed with you and agreed in writing before proceeding.

What is not included?

The fee does not cover optional IV sedation (£600) or unrelated dental work identified during consultation. General dentistry, fillings, or root canal treatment on other teeth are priced separately.

Do you offer insurance-assisted treatment?

Implant treatment is not usually covered by standard UK dental insurance. Some private health policies cover portions. We provide full itemised invoices to support any reimbursement claim.

What happens if an implant fails?

Our five-year success rate is 98%. In the rare event of early implant failure (within 12 months) we replace the implant at no additional cost. This is written into your treatment agreement.
