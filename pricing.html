Factors That Affect Your Investment

Several factors influence the final cost of your treatment:

1. Number of Implants

All-on-4: Four implants per arch. Suitable for most cases with adequate bone.
All-on-6: Six implants per arch. Provides additional stability and may be recommended for certain cases.

2. Tooth Extractions

If you have remaining teeth that need removal, this is included in your treatment plan. The number and complexity of extractions may affect the overall cost slightly.

3. Bone Grafting

Most patients do not need bone grafting with the All-on-4 or All-on-6 technique. However, in cases of significant bone loss, minor grafting may be required: Â£500-Â£2,000 per site if needed.

Is Dental Implant Treatment Tax Deductible?

Dental implants are generally not tax-deductible in the UK, as they're typically considered a private medical expense rather than a business expense. However, if you're self-employed and can demonstrate that improved appearance is essential for your business, you may be able to claim a portion. We recommend consulting with your accountant for specific advice.

Insurance Coverage

Most NHS and standard dental insurance plans do not cover full arch dental implants, as they're considered cosmetic or elective rather than medically necessary (even though they provide significant health benefits).

However, some private dental insurance plans offer partial coverage. We recommend:

  • Reviewing your policy carefully
  • Contacting your insurer to ask about implant coverage
  • Asking if they cover any portion (consultation, imaging, etc.)

We're happy to provide any documentation your insurance company requires.

  • Hidden costs: Some advertised prices don't include imaging, provisional teeth, or final teeth

Pricing Questions Answered

Why do dental implants cost so much?

The cost reflects specialist expertise, surgical precision, high-quality materials, advanced technology, and comprehensive care over several months. You're paying for a solution that can last 20+ years.

Can I pay monthly?

Yes. We offer 0% interest plans over 12 months and extended payment plans up to 5 years to make treatment affordable.

Is the consultation really free?

Yes, completely free with no obligation. You'll receive a full examination, 3D scan, and detailed treatment plan with pricing.

Are there any hidden costs?

No. The price we quote includes everything from consultation through to final teeth and aftercare. What we quote is what you pay.

What if I can't afford the full amount?

We offer flexible finance plans to spread the cost. During your consultation, we'll discuss options that fit your budget.

Do you offer payment plans for people with poor credit?

We work with multiple finance providers who consider applications on a case-by-case basis. We'll help you explore all available options.

Can I use my dental insurance?

It depends on your policy. Most NHS and basic plans don't cover implants, but some private plans offer partial coverage. Check with your insurer.

What's the difference between your prices and cheaper overseas options?

Our prices include specialist expertise, premium materials, comprehensive aftercare, and local convenience. Overseas treatment often has hidden costs (travel, time off, follow-up difficulties) and may use less experienced clinicians or lower-quality materials.

Do I pay upfront or at the end?

We offer flexible options. You can pay a deposit to secure your treatment date, with the balance due before or on your surgery day, or use one of our finance plans.

What happens if something goes wrong after treatment?

All necessary follow-up care in the first year is included. If complications arise due to the treatment, we'll address them at no additional cost. Your treatment plan will detail our warranty terms.
