Your Journey to Fixed Teeth

A clear, step-by-step guide to what happens from your first consultation through to your beautiful new smile.

What to Expect: Your Complete Journey

We understand that considering full arch dental implants is a big decision. One of the best ways to feel confident is to know exactly what to expect at every stage.

This guide walks you through the entire process, from your very first phone call to your long-term care. There are no surprises‚Äîjust a clear, transparent journey to your new smile.

Timeline Overview

  • Week 1: Initial consultation and planning
  • Week 2-4: Treatment planning and preparation
  • Surgery Day: Implants placed, temporary teeth fitted
  • Months 1-6: Healing and integration
  • Month 6+: Final teeth fitted
  • Ongoing: Regular maintenance appointments

Stage 1: Initial Enquiry

Your journey begins with a simple phone call or online enquiry. When you contact us, you'll speak with a friendly member of our team who will:

  • Listen to your concerns and what you're hoping to achieve
  • Answer any initial questions you have
  • Explain the consultation process
  • Book you in for a free, no-obligation consultation
  • Send you information to read before your appointment

There's no pressure, no sales pitch‚Äîjust helpful information to prepare you for your consultation.

Stage 2: Free Consultation

Duration: 45-60 minutes

Your consultation is comprehensive and gives us the information we need to recommend the best treatment for you.

What Happens During Your Consultation:

1. Discussion (15-20 minutes)

We'll talk about your current situation, what's bothering you, and what you're hoping to achieve. This is your opportunity to ask questions and voice any concerns.

2. Clinical Examination (10-15 minutes)

A thorough examination of your teeth, gums, bite, and oral health. We'll check for any issues that need addressing.

3. 3D CT Scan (5 minutes)

A quick, painless 3D scan that gives us detailed information about your bone density and anatomy. This is essential for planning your treatment.

4. Treatment Discussion (15-20 minutes)

We'll review your scan together, explain your options, discuss what's achievable, and answer all your questions.

5. Written Quote

Before you leave, you'll receive a comprehensive written treatment plan and transparent pricing. No hidden costs, no surprises.

After Your Consultation

Take your time to think things over. There's absolutely no pressure to make an immediate decision. We're here to answer questions whenever you need us.

Stage 3: Treatment Planning

Duration: 1-2 weeks

Once you decide to proceed, we begin detailed treatment planning:

  • Digital Smile Design: Using software, we design your new smile based on your facial features and preferences
  • Surgical Planning: We determine the exact position, angle, and depth of each implant
  • Temporary Teeth Creation: We fabricate your temporary fixed teeth to be fitted on surgery day
  • Pre-operative Appointment: Review your treatment plan, answer final questions, and prepare for surgery

Stage 4: Surgery Day

Duration: 2-4 hours per arch

This is the day you've been waiting for. Here's exactly what happens:

Before Surgery

  • Arrive at the practice (we recommend bringing someone to accompany you home)
  • Complete any final paperwork
  • Discuss sedation options if chosen
  • Local anaesthetic is administered‚Äîyou'll be completely numb and comfortable

During Surgery

The procedure follows these steps:

  1. Preparation: Any remaining teeth that need extraction are removed
  2. Implant Placement: 4-6 implants are placed using guided surgery techniques for precision
  3. Immediate Teeth: Your temporary fixed bridge is attached to the implants
  4. Final Checks: We ensure everything fits properly and comfortably

Throughout the procedure, you're kept comfortable and informed. Most patients are surprised by how straightforward it is.

Immediately After Surgery

You'll leave the practice with your new fixed teeth in place. You'll receive:

  • Detailed aftercare instructions
  • Pain medication and antibiotics if needed
  • Emergency contact number
  • Follow-up appointment scheduled

Stage 5: Initial Recovery

Duration: 1-2 weeks

First 24-48 Hours

  • Rest at home‚Äîtake it easy
  • Apply ice packs to reduce swelling
  • Eat soft, cool foods
  • Take prescribed medications as directed
  • Avoid strenuous activity

First Week

  • Swelling and bruising are normal and will subside
  • Stick to soft foods (mashed potato, soup, yoghurt, scrambled eggs)
  • Gently clean your teeth as instructed
  • Most patients return to work after 3-5 days

Week 2 Onwards

  • Gradually introduce more normal foods
  • Swelling should be mostly resolved
  • Continue with good oral hygiene
  • Attend your 2-week check-up

Stage 6: Healing Period

Duration: 3-6 months

During this phase, your implants are integrating with your bone‚Äîa process called osseointegration. This creates the strong foundation for your final teeth.

What to Expect:

  • You'll wear your comfortable temporary fixed teeth
  • Gradually return to normal eating (avoiding very hard foods)
  • Attend periodic check-ups (typically at 2 weeks, 6 weeks, and 3 months)
  • Your temporary teeth may be adjusted for optimal comfort
  • By month 3, you'll feel completely normal

Life During Healing

Most patients adapt quickly to their temporary teeth. You can work, socialize, and live normally. Many people report that even the temporary teeth feel better than their dentures ever did.

Stage 7: Final Teeth Fitting

Duration: 2-3 appointments over 2-4 weeks

Once your implants have fully integrated, it's time for your permanent teeth.

The Process:

Appointment 1: Impressions & Design

We take detailed impressions and measurements. You'll help design your final smile‚Äîchoosing the shade, shape, and size of your teeth.

Appointment 2: Try-In

You'll try your new teeth and we'll make any adjustments needed to ensure they're perfect.

Appointment 3: Final Fitting

Your beautiful, permanent teeth are fitted and secured. You'll leave with your complete new smile.

Stage 8: Ongoing Care

Your implants can last for decades with proper care. Here's what you need to do:

Daily Care at Home:

  • Brush your teeth twice daily
  • Floss or use interdental brushes daily
  • Use a water flosser if recommended
  • Maintain good overall oral hygiene

Regular Check-ups:

  • First year: Appointments at 3, 6, and 12 months
  • After first year: Annual check-ups and professional cleaning
  • We'll check implant health, clean your teeth, and address any issues

Common Questions About the Journey

Will I be without teeth at any point?

No. You'll receive temporary fixed teeth on the same day as your surgery. You're never without teeth.

How much pain should I expect?

Most patients report less discomfort than expected. The first few days may involve some soreness and swelling, but this is typically manageable with over-the-counter pain relief. Many patients say it's less painful than a tooth extraction.

When can I return to work?

Most patients return to work within 3-5 days. If your job is physically demanding, you may need a week.

Can I eat normally?

You'll start with soft foods and gradually progress to normal eating over 2-3 months. Once your final teeth are fitted, you can eat virtually anything.

What if something goes wrong?

Complications are rare, but we're always here if you need us. You'll have an emergency contact number and can reach us anytime during your recovery.
