Your Journey to Fixed Teeth

A clear, step-by-step guide to what happens from your first consultation through to your beautiful new smile.

What to Expect: Your Complete Journey

We understand that considering full arch dental implants is a big decision. One of the best ways to feel confident is to know exactly what to expect at every stage.

This guide walks you through the entire process, from your very first phone call to your long-term care. We believe transparency removes anxiety and helps you make informed decisions.

The typical timeline from consultation to final teeth is 4-6 months, though you'll leave surgery with a beautiful provisional smile the same day.

Stage 1: Initial Contact & Consultation Booking

Making Contact

Your journey begins when you reach out to us, either by:

  • Calling us at 01202 555 555
  • Filling in our online contact form
  • Emailing us at info@bmthimplants.co.uk

When you contact us, our friendly reception team will:

  • Answer any initial questions you have
  • Explain what happens at the consultation
  • Find a convenient appointment time for you
  • Send you confirmation and directions
  • Provide a medical history form to complete before your visit

Timeline: We typically have consultation appointments available within 1-2 weeks.

Stage 2: Free Initial Consultation

Duration: 45-60 minutes
Cost: Free, no obligation

What Happens at Your Consultation

1

Welcome & Medical History Review

We'll review your medical history form, discuss any current medications, and ensure dental implants are safe for you. This is also your chance to tell us about your concerns and goals.

Duration: 10 minutes

2

Clinical Examination

We'll examine your mouth, teeth, and gums. We'll assess the condition of any remaining teeth, check your bite, and look for any issues that need to be addressed.

Duration: 15 minutes

3

3D Imaging (CBCT Scan)

We'll take a 3D X-ray of your jaws. This painless scan gives us detailed information about your bone quality, density, and structure. It takes just 30 seconds and uses very low radiation.

Duration: 5 minutes

4

Discussion & Education

We'll explain your options, show you how implants work, and discuss whether you're a suitable candidate. We'll use your scans to illustrate the treatment plan and answer all your questions.

Duration: 20 minutes

5

Treatment Plan & Pricing

You'll receive a detailed written treatment plan outlining recommended procedures, timeline, and exact costs. We'll explain finance options if you're interested.

You take this home - no pressure to decide today

What to Bring to Your Consultation

  • Completed medical history form (we'll send this in advance)
  • List of current medications
  • Details of any medical conditions
  • Your questions written down
  • A family member or friend for support (optional but encouraged)
  • Recent dental X-rays if you have them (optional)

No Obligation, No Pressure

This consultation is about education and exploration. You won't be pressured to make a decision on the day. Take your treatment plan home, think about it, discuss it with family, and contact us when you're readyâ€”or not at all. There's no commitment.

Stage 3: Decision & Treatment Planning

Timeline: Take as long as you need

After Your Consultation

Take time to review your treatment plan and consider your options. Many patients find it helpful to:

  • Discuss the plan with family
  • Research dental implants online
  • Read patient testimonials
  • Review the financial commitment
  • Explore finance options

When you're ready to proceed, simply contact us. We'll schedule your next appointment for detailed treatment planning.

Pre-Treatment Planning Appointment

Duration: 60-90 minutes
What happens:

  • Digital impressions of your teeth and gums
  • Photographs of your smile from multiple angles
  • Bite registration to understand your jaw relationship
  • Digital smile designâ€”see a preview of your new teeth
  • Finalize your treatment plan
  • Schedule your surgery date
  • Arrange finance if needed

This appointment ensures everything is planned to perfection before your surgery day.

Stage 4: Pre-Surgery Preparation

Timeline: 1-2 weeks before surgery

What You'll Receive

  • Detailed pre-surgery instructions
  • Prescription for post-surgery medications (collected in advance)
  • List of what to eat and avoid before surgery
  • Information about sedation if you've chosen this option
  • Confirmation of your surgery date and arrival time
  • Emergency contact numbers

Preparing for Surgery Day

The Day Before

  • Eat a good meal the evening before
  • Get a good night's sleep
  • Pick up your prescribed medications from the pharmacy
  • Arrange for someone to drive you home
  • Prepare soft foods for your recovery period

On Surgery Day

  • Eat a light breakfast (unless having IV sedation)
  • Take any regular medications as directed
  • Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing
  • Arrive 15 minutes before your appointment
  • Bring someone to drive you home

Stage 5: Surgery Day - Implant Placement

Duration: 2-4 hours
Result: You leave with fixed provisional teeth the same day

What Happens on Surgery Day

1

Arrival & Preparation (30 minutes)

You'll be welcomed, settled into our comfortable surgical suite, and prepared for the procedure. If you've chosen sedation, this will be administered by a qualified anaesthetist.

2

Anaesthesia & Comfort

Local anaesthetic is administered to ensure you're completely comfortable. If you've chosen sedation, you'll be relaxed and calm but still able to respond to instructions.

3

Extractions (if needed)

If you have any remaining teeth that need removal, we'll do this first. You won't feel any painâ€”just pressure.

4

Implant Placement (60-90 minutes)

Using our computer-guided surgical technique, we'll place 4-6 implants into precise positions in your jawbone. Each implant acts as an artificial tooth root.

5

Provisional Teeth Attachment

Immediately after implant placement, we'll attach your provisional (temporary) fixed teeth. These are fully functional and look natural. You'll be able to see and smile with your new teeth right away.

6

Recovery & Post-Op Instructions

You'll rest for 15-30 minutes while the anaesthetic wears off. We'll provide detailed aftercare instructions, answer questions, and schedule your follow-up appointment.

What You'll Experience

During surgery:

  • No pain (anaesthetic ensures this)
  • Pressure and pushing sensations
  • Sounds of instruments and equipment
  • If sedated: drowsiness and deep relaxation

After surgery (same day):

  • Numbness for 2-4 hours after surgery
  • Minor swelling (peaks day 2-3)
  • Some bruising may appear
  • Mild to moderate discomfort (managed with pain relief)
  • Most importantly: You have your new teeth!

Stage 6: Recovery & Healing Period

Timeline: 3-6 months
What's happening: Your implants are integrating with your jawbone (osseointegration)

The First Week

Days 1-3: Initial Recovery

  • Rest at home as much as possible
  • Apply ice packs to reduce swelling
  • Eat soft, cool foods (soup, smoothies, mashed potatoes)
  • Take prescribed pain medication as directed
  • Keep your head elevated when sleeping
  • Avoid strenuous activity

Most patients return to work after 2-3 days

Days 4-7: Improving Comfort

  • Swelling starts to subside
  • You can gradually introduce more foods
  • Begin gentle brushing around your new teeth
  • Resume normal activities (but no heavy exercise yet)

Follow-Up Appointments

We'll see you for regular check-ups during the healing period:

  • 1 week: Check healing, remove stitches if needed, adjust provisional teeth if necessary
  • 2 weeks: Confirm healing is progressing well
  • 6 weeks: Check implant stability
  • 3 months: Assess integration progress
  • 4-6 months: Confirm implants are fully integrated and ready for final teeth

Living with Provisional Teeth

During the healing period, you'll wear your provisional (temporary) fixed teeth. These are:

  • Fully functionalâ€”you can eat and speak normally
  • Natural-lookingâ€”you can smile with confidence
  • Fixed in placeâ€”they don't come out
  • Carefully designed to protect your implants during healing

What You Can Eat

Weeks 1-2: Soft foods only (soup, eggs, soft pasta, smoothies)

Weeks 3-6: Gradually introduce firmer foods (cooked vegetables, fish, tender meats)

Months 2-6: Most foods are fine, avoiding very hard or sticky items

Caring for Your Provisional Teeth

  • Brush gently twice daily with a soft toothbrush
  • Use an antimicrobial mouthwash as directed
  • Clean around the implants with interdental brushes
  • Attend all follow-up appointments
  • Report any concerns immediately

Stage 7: Final Teeth Fabrication

Timeline: Months 4-6
Duration: 2-3 appointments over 3-4 weeks

Creating Your Permanent Teeth

1

Final Impressions

Once your implants are fully integrated, we'll take detailed impressions and photographs. We'll also verify your bite and discuss the appearance of your final teeth.

2

Try-In Appointment

We'll fit a prototype version of your final teeth to check fit, function, and aesthetics. This is your opportunity to provide feedback before finalization.

3

Final Fitting

Your beautiful, permanent teeth are fitted. We'll make any final adjustments to ensure perfect comfort, function, and appearance.

Your Final Teeth

Your permanent teeth are custom-crafted from high-strength materials designed to last decades. They're created to match your facial features, skin tone, and personal preferences. The result is a smile that looks completely natural and feels like your own teeth.

Stage 8: Ongoing Care & Maintenance

Timeline: Lifelong
Goal: Keep your implants healthy for 20+ years

Home Care

  • Brush twice daily with a soft toothbrush
  • Use interdental brushes or a water flosser around implants
  • Use antimicrobial mouthwash as recommended
  • Avoid smoking (it compromises implant health)

Professional Maintenance

We'll see you every 6 months for:

  • Professional cleaning
  • Implant and tissue health assessment
  • X-rays to check bone levels
  • Adjustments if needed
  • Early identification of any issues

These appointments are crucial for long-term success and are typically included in your first year of treatment.
