What is a Prosthodontist?

A prosthodontist is a dental specialist who has completed additional years of training beyond general dentistry, focusing specifically on restoring and replacing teeth. Think of prosthodontics as the architectural and engineering side of dentistry—we specialize in designing and implementing complex full-mouth rehabilitations.

While general dentists can place implants, prosthodontists are trained to handle the most complex cases, including:

  • Full arch implant rehabilitation (All-on-4, All-on-6)
  • Complex bite reconstruction
  • Aesthetic smile design
  • Management of compromised or failing dentitions
  • Treatment planning for extensive reconstruction

Harvard Training: What It Means for You

Harvard School of Dental Medicine is consistently ranked among the top dental schools globally. Their prosthodontics program is renowned for:

  • Evidence-based approach: Treatment decisions are based on the latest peer-reviewed research, not trends or anecdotal evidence
  • Rigorous clinical training: Extensive hands-on experience with complex full arch cases
  • Multidisciplinary collaboration: Working alongside specialists from periodontics, oral surgery, and other fields
  • Advanced biomaterials science: Deep understanding of how implants and materials interact with bone and tissue
  • Digital dentistry leadership: Early adoption and mastery of 3D planning and guided surgery

This training means you benefit from techniques and standards of care developed and refined at one of the world's leading academic medical centers.

Our Technology & Facility

We've invested in advanced technology to provide the most precise, comfortable, and predictable treatment possible.

3D Cone Beam CT Scanner

High-resolution 3D imaging allows us to see your jaw structure in detail, assess bone quality, identify nerve locations, and plan implant placement with sub-millimeter precision.

Digital Impression System

No messy impression putty. Our intraoral scanner captures digital impressions that are more accurate and far more comfortable for you.

Computer-Guided Surgery

We design your surgery virtually, then use surgical guides to place implants exactly as planned. This increases accuracy and reduces surgery time.

Digital Smile Design

See a preview of your new smile before treatment begins. We can show you digitally how your final teeth will look, and make adjustments based on your feedback.

In-House Surgical Suite

Our dedicated surgical environment meets strict infection control standards and is designed for your comfort and safety.

Modern Sterilization

We exceed GDC sterilization standards with validated processes, ensuring every instrument is sterile and every surface is safe.

Questions About Our Practice

What makes you different from other dentists?

We are prosthodontists who specialize exclusively in full arch implant rehabilitation. This focused expertise, combined with Harvard-level training, means we handle complex cases with confidence and experience.

Do you accept NHS patients?

We are a private specialist practice. Full arch dental implants are not available on the NHS. However, we offer flexible payment plans to make treatment accessible.

Will I see the same dentist every time?

Yes. You'll see the same specialist prosthodontist throughout your treatment journey, ensuring continuity of care and a personal relationship.

How do I know if I can trust you?

We're fully GDC registered, maintain professional indemnity insurance, and our credentials can be independently verified. We also encourage you to read our patient testimonials and book a free consultation to meet us.

Do you treat nervous patients?

Absolutely. Many of our patients have dental anxiety. We offer sedation options and take extra time to ensure you feel comfortable and in control throughout treatment.

Can I bring a family member to my consultation?

Yes, we encourage it. Having someone with you for support and to help remember information is always welcome.
