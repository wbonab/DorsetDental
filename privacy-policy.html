§ 01Who we are

Bournemouth Dental Implants is a branding name used by Bright Blue Dental Practice ("we", "us", "our"), a single dental practice operating from 233 Charminster Road, Bournemouth, BH8 9QJ. Bournemouth Dental Implants is not a separate legal entity — it is the public-facing name for the implant service we deliver from the same premises and under the same registration. We are registered with the Care Quality Commission and regulated by the General Dental Council.

For the purposes of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018, we are the data controller of any personal information you share with us.

§ 02Information we collect

We collect personal information in the course of providing dental care, responding to enquiries and operating this website. Categories include:

From patients

  • Identity and contact details (name, date of birth, address, phone, email)
  • Medical and dental history, including medications, allergies and clinical notes
  • Radiographic images, intraoral photography and 3D scans
  • Treatment plans, fee estimates and payment records
  • Records of communication between you and the practice

From website visitors and enquirers

  • Information you submit through the booking or contact form
  • Approximate location, device type and browser, from analytics cookies (with your consent)
  • Communications you send to us by email or telephone

§ 03How we use your information

We use personal information to:

  • Provide dental treatment and ongoing clinical care
  • Maintain accurate clinical records as required by law
  • Communicate with you about appointments, treatment and follow-up
  • Issue invoices, take payment and administer finance arrangements
  • Respond to enquiries made through the website, telephone or email
  • Comply with our legal, regulatory and clinical-governance obligations
  • Improve the quality of the service and the website

We do not use your personal information for marketing without your explicit consent, and we never sell it.

§ 04Lawful basis for processing

We rely on the following lawful bases under the UK GDPR:

  • Contract: processing necessary to provide the dental services you have requested.
  • Legal obligation: retention of clinical records, infection-control records and other documents required by NHS, CQC and GDC regulations.
  • Legitimate interests: running the practice efficiently, responding to enquiries and maintaining the security of our systems.
  • Consent: use of non-essential cookies, marketing communications and any use of clinical photography for non-clinical purposes.

Where we process special-category data (health information), we additionally rely on Article 9(2)(h) of the UK GDPR — processing necessary for the provision of healthcare.

§ 05Sharing your information

We share information only where necessary and with appropriate safeguards. The main recipients are:

  • Clinicians and clinical staff involved in your care
  • Referring or onward-referral dentists, with your consent
  • Dental laboratories and implant providers that fabricate components for your treatment
  • Our practice-management software, secure imaging and accounting providers (acting as processors under written agreement)
  • Regulatory bodies (GDC, CQC) and our insurers and indemnity providers where legally required
  • Finance providers, where you choose to take finance for treatment

We do not transfer personal data outside the UK except where doing so is necessary for a specific cloud service, and in those cases we rely on UK-approved standard contractual clauses.

§ 06How long we keep records

Clinical records are retained in line with NHS and Department of Health guidance:

  • Adult patients: at least 11 years from the date of the most recent entry, or until the patient's 25th birthday, whichever is longer.
  • Patients under 18: until their 25th birthday, or 26th if treatment ended at age 17.
  • Radiographs and 3D imaging: retained for the same period as the clinical record.
  • Enquiries that do not result in treatment: 24 months from last contact.
  • Financial records: 7 years, to meet HMRC requirements.

§ 07Your rights

Under the UK GDPR you have the right to:

  • Be informed about how your data is used (this notice)
  • Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Have inaccurate data corrected
  • Request erasure of data, where there is no overriding legal duty to retain it
  • Restrict or object to certain processing
  • Withdraw consent at any time where consent is the lawful basis
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at brightbluedental@gmail.com. We will respond within one calendar month.

§ 08Security

We take the confidentiality of your records seriously. Measures we operate include:

  • Encrypted, access-controlled clinical software
  • UK-based, ISO 27001-aligned cloud hosting for clinical and imaging data
  • Strict access on a need-to-know basis among practice staff
  • Secure destruction of paper records once their retention period ends
  • Mandatory information-governance and GDPR training for all staff

§ 09Cookies & website analytics

This website uses a small number of cookies — see our Cookie policy for the full list. Essential cookies are used to operate the booking form. Analytics cookies are only set where you have given consent.
